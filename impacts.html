Step 03 // The harm

Who Gets
Harmed

The consumption from Step 02 doesn’t disappear; it lands on real people, water, air, and land. And much of it is deliberately hidden until the deal is already done.

The documented impacts

WATER

Local water stress

Facilities sited in already-strained regions compete with residents and agriculture for drinking water, and can push utility costs upward for everyone nearby.

Source: S&P Global →

GRID

Higher bills & dirtier power

Grid-upgrade costs are often passed to residential ratepayers, while sudden demand can keep aging fossil-fuel plants online longer than planned.

Source: World Resources Institute →

HEALTH

Noise & infrasound

The constant low-frequency hum of industrial cooling fans and backup gas turbines is linked to sleep disruption and chronic stress for nearby neighborhoods.

Source: Tom’s Hardware →

LAND

Land & ecology

Farmland and open land rezoned to heavy industrial use permanently alters local ecology, character, and future land options for a community.

Source: Lincoln Institute →

EQUITY

Unequal burden

The costs and risks frequently fall hardest on rural and frontline communities, the people with the least say in where these facilities go.

Source: Kapor Foundation →

E-WASTE

Disposable hardware

High-performance AI chips are often obsolete within a few years, driving a fast-growing stream of electronic waste and constant demand for new critical minerals.

Source: Princeton CITP →

The secrecy problem

What makes all of this harder to fight is that it’s often invisible until it’s too late. This is the single most important thing to understand before Step 04.

The Transparency Crisis

Tech giants frequently route projects through shell companies with placeholder code-names, and push local governments into non-disclosure agreements (NDAs). The result: residents often don’t learn what’s being built, or how much water and power it will take, until the contracts are already signed. Weaponized secrecy bypasses public scrutiny by design.

Here is the hopeful part, and the hinge of this whole guide: secrecy is also a weakness. Because these deals depend on hiding information and on local approvals, the legal tools that force disclosure and control land use are exactly where an informed community can win. That’s Step 04.
