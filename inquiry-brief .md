# Inquiry Brief: Digital Sovereignty

## Project Title
Digital Sovereignty: Arming Communities Against AI Data Extraction

## 1. Curiosity Statement & The Pivot
My objective is to expose the physical threats of AI data centers and translate foundational local laws into tactical visual tools that empower communities to block extractive corporate infrastructure. 

Originally, this inquiry began by investigating corporate "Green AI" and how tech companies could shift to sustainable energy. However, discovering the **Jevons Paradox** fundamentally changed the trajectory of the project. The paradox proves that as AI infrastructure becomes more energy-efficient, corporations simply scale their operations, completely wiping out any environmental savings. Recognizing that corporate sustainability reports often mask the true scale of resource extraction, I pivoted the project to address the root cause. The goal is no longer to wait for corporate transparency, but to equip local citizens with the legal and visual tools they need to demand it and protect their own resources.

## 2. The Core Research Question
How can we translate the physical hazards of AI infrastructure (grid strain, water depletion, and noise pollution) and local legal choke points (zoning moratoriums, FOIA, and PUC interventions) into an accessible, interactive visual toolkit that equips citizens to proactively defend their land against Big Tech?

## 3. Why It Matters (The Real-World Threat)
The conversation around AI often treats it as an invisible, digital cloud. In reality, it is a hyper-extractive physical industry that directly targets local communities. 
*   **The Transparency Crisis:** Tech giants actively hide their resource consumption behind shell companies (e.g., "Project Bigfoot") and force local municipalities into Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs). This weaponized secrecy bypasses public scrutiny until deals are already finalized.
*   **Water Depletion:** Data centers use millions of gallons of potable drinking water daily for cooling, leading to local water stress and higher utility bills for surrounding residents.
*   **Grid Strain:** The massive energy pull of AI infrastructure forces local utility companies to keep dirty coal plants running longer and passes the cost of massive grid upgrades down to residential ratepayers.
*   **Infrasound Pollution:** The constant low-frequency hum from thousands of industrial HVAC fans and backup gas turbines causes documented public health hazards, including sleep deprivation and chronic stress for nearby neighborhoods.

## 4. The Tactical Arsenal (Community Power)
This project operates on the fact that Big Tech cannot bypass local zoning. Under the 10th Amendment, the federal government does not control local land use. Therefore, the ultimate legal power rests with local city councils and informed citizens. The toolkit will visualize the following defense strategies:
*   **Zoning Moratoriums:** Teaching communities how to petition for temporary legal bans on new data centers while environmental impacts are studied, or how to demand reclassification from "light commercial" to "heavy industrial."
*   **Breaking NDAs via FOIA:** Providing step-by-step guides on using Freedom of Information Act requests to legally demand the release of water usage requests and tax break proposals hidden by shell companies.
*   **Public Utility Commission (PUC) Interventions:** Educating citizens on how to file as "intervenors" to legally force utility companies to assign infrastructure costs directly to the data centers rather than residential households.

## 5. Design & Execution Strategy
To make this dense legal and environmental information accessible, the visual execution must be highly intentional.
*   **Progressive Disclosure:** The interface will utilize a step-by-step learning structure, similar to educational platforms like Khan Academy. Because the target audience may have zero prior knowledge of AI infrastructure, information will be revealed logically through timeline cards and accordions rather than massive text dumps.
*   **Authoritative Aesthetic:** The design will use a light, high-contrast palette with stark typography to feel authoritative, trustworthy, and serious. It will avoid chaotic "hacker" aesthetics or generic tech UI trends.
*   **Authentic Visual Evidence:** The project strictly rejects the use of AI-generated imagery. To emphasize the physical reality of the threat, the site will exclusively feature real-world, verified photography of data center interiors, cooling towers, and documented community impacts.
*   **Authorship & AI Collaboration:** While AI coding agents are used to generate the structural HTML and CSS, the design intent, content hierarchy, and boundary-setting remain strictly manual. The AI is treated as a coding assistant, not an art director.
