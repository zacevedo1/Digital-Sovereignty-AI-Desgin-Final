# Project Interview Notes

## Project Title
Digital Sovereignty: Arming Communities Against AI Data Extraction

## One-Sentence Project Summary
My final project is an interactive, tactical web toolkit designed to empower local communities with the legal and visual resources they need to fight the extractive physical footprint (water, grid, and noise) of corporate AI data centers.

## Starting Point
At the beginning of the quarter, my original starting point was much broader and more passive. I was investigating "Green AI Data Centers" and looking at how tech corporations could shift to sustainable energy. 

## Inquiry Question
How can we translate the physical hazards of AI infrastructure and local legal choke points into an interactive, visual toolkit that equips citizens to proactively defend their land against Big Tech?

## Why This Question Mattered
This question mattered because the physical threat is tangible. AI data centers bring severe local water stress, force residential power rate hikes, and cause documented public health hazards. Tech giants actively hide these impacts behind shell companies and NDAs. Unless dense legal rights are visualized and made frictionless, communities will continue to be steamrolled.

## Research and Sources
A fundamental source for my project was the Microsoft Sustainability Report. Even knowing that corporate data can be spun or inaccurate, it provided a necessary baseline and structure. 

## What I Found
I found that even if sustainability reports are later proven inaccurate, they give a clear picture of the sheer scale of the infrastructure. Analyzing these polished reports taught me exactly what to look for when identifying false numbers, what the goals of an AI company are, and how to spot greenwashing.

## Source Verification
I approached source verification by intentionally looking at corporate data not just as fact, but as a structure to be reverse-engineered. 

## How Research Shaped the Project
Discovering the Jevons Paradox completely reframed my research. It showed me why the tech sector has not truly shifted to green AI. Even as infrastructure becomes more efficient, corporations scale their operations so rapidly that it wipes out environmental savings. This realization shifted my focus away from waiting for corporate sustainability and toward building local legal defense tools.

## Research Dashboard / Approach and Engagement
The dashboard will display my approach by contrasting the polished corporate data against the raw physical realities of extraction, proving why local intervention is necessary.

## AI Tools and Prompt Experiments
My prompting strategy evolved by intentionally seeking outside perspectives. I brought my research to my partner, my instructor, and peers outside the design studio to get a sharper understanding of how to steer the AI. I learned to force the tool to help me design for structural change from the root cause, rather than letting it guide the project with surface-level ideas.

## Process Page / Workflow and Pivots
The biggest pivot occurred when I stopped trying to solve the problem from the outside. I realized that if the root process of digital economic growth does not change, nothing changes. 

## Final Product or Outcome
The final product is a "Tactical Terminal" website featuring a Corporate Transparency Scorecard, a physical threat index, and an interactive FOIA generator.

## Product Page / Authorship and Synthesis
I asserted my authorship by strictly managing the AI coding agent's focus. I frequently used the AI to analyze data out of curiosity, but when the AI attempted to integrate those tangents into the actual project, I explicitly told it "no." For the visual direction, I fed the AI a lot of visual references. The AI suggested the specific term "Tactical Terminal," which perfectly captured the design intent I already had but lacked the exact words for.

## What I Learned
I learned that you have to pick at an issue directly from its core. You must steer AI intentionally, setting firm boundaries to keep the project concentrated on its primary goal.

## Reflection Notes
If I had another 10 weeks to develop this platform, I would expand it to include a live, localized intelligence system. This would feature an early-warning news feed for data center scouting, an interactive map for residents to report real-time negative impacts, and a direct directory connecting users to active grassroots advocacy groups.

## Evidence to Include
Ironically, even though the website structure is AI generated, I explicitly refuse to use any AI generated images. People form deeper connections with real life photography, especially given the current backlash against generative AI art. To expose the true physical impact of this infrastructure, I will only use real, verified photos of the insides of actual data centers and real life documentation of their environmental impacts.
